# LocalClaw — Logo Design Philosophy

## Brand Identity

LocalClaw is a managed AI agent service. The brand communicates executive-grade intelligence: controlled, precise, and powerful. It is not a startup playfully experimenting with AI — it is the infrastructure that serious operators trust.

## Core Visual Language

**The Claw as Metaphor**

The central mark is a claw — three or four curved slash marks radiating from a single convergence point, like the moment of a predator's strike frozen in geometry. Each stroke tapers sharply from a broad base to a near-zero tip, communicating:

- Precision over brute force
- Decisiveness — a single, committed action
- Speed and intelligence combined

The claw is not literal or illustrative. It is constructed from clean geometric arcs. It reads as abstract enough to feel modern and versatile, while the reference is unmistakable.

**Containment + Control**

The mark sits within a subtle circular field — a thin ring that contains and frames the claw without caging it. This communicates bounded power: the AI operates within defined parameters, under your control. The containment ring also ensures the mark reads cleanly at favicon scale.

## Color System

| Token | Hex | Role |
|-------|-----|------|
| Obsidian | `#080704` | Background — depth, authority |
| Sovereign Gold | `#C9922A` | Primary mark — value, precision |
| Warm Cream | `#F5EED8` | Typography — legibility, warmth |

Gold on near-black is the language of premium: watchmakers, private banks, executive hardware. It signals that the product is not free-tier.

## Typography

**Wordmark:** Big Shoulders Bold (or similar high-contrast geometric sans)
- All-caps tracking communicates confidence without aggression
- The condensed letterform pairs with the vertical energy of the claw

**Supporting text:** Instrument Sans Regular
- Clean, modern, readable at all sizes

## Construction Principles

1. **No gradients on the claw strokes** — the mark must reproduce at 1-color in any context
2. **Minimum viable detail** — nothing that disappears at 16×16px
3. **Optical centering** — the claw convergence point sits slightly below visual center, giving the mark upward energy
4. **Proportional consistency** — stroke weight at base is exactly 1/8 of canvas width; taper ratio is 12:1

## What to Avoid

- Gradients that simulate 3D depth (cheap, dates quickly)
- Drop shadows on the claw itself
- More than 4 strokes (loses precision, gains noise)
- Any glow effect that wasn't specifically specified
- Rounded stroke caps (use flat or slightly angled)
- The word "claw" as a visual pun — the geometry speaks for itself
